## Robots

First of all what is a robot?
This is my personal definition:

> A robot is a man made mechanical and electronic device made to perform one or more tasks.

From that definition, I split robots into two different categories:
- Passive robots
- Active or autonomous robots

### Passive robots

Passive robots are robots that do not act unless they are powered on and explicitly instructed to operate
in the specific mode desired by its user. The command **always** comes from the user and the action **always** performed
by the robot.

In that category, we found most of our existing appliances from washing machine to coffee machine etc.
Thos are the appliances that most people have no issue with. Maybe because they can command them but also because those 
robots are performing trivial tasks that is not associated with a particular job anymore.

Historically, people used to wash their clothes by themselves, but nowadays in most places on earth, a washing machine -
sometimes coupled with a dryer - is used to do all the work.

### Active or autonomous robots

Active or autonomous robots are the robots who are able to operate autonomously. They can be programmed to perform the task
under certain circumstances, but the **decision** to perform the task is always done by the robots themselves, based on the 
conditions imposed by the user.

Those are the autonomous trains, robot vacuum cleaner or again Spot the robot dog from Boston Dynamics.
Those autonomous entities are serving their purpose autonomously and unless the circumstances of their operation need to be changed
they can carry their mission successfully without any human intervention (including their charging operation).

Thos are the robots that are the least accepted by most people because they can threat some people's jobs.
They can be compared in some ways with the A.I agents in the digital space (internet) : A.I agents for the physical space.
Those are the robots, I believe, that we need the most in the future as we move toward a more modern and robot-friendly world with its infrastructure
made and tailored for autonomous robots to operate safely and efficiently.

## Why do we need more bots

That is an important question. Why do we need them after all?
First, we would like to live in a safer and more secure world.
That sentiment is shared by everyone including the very people who are not keen on having more bots at the moment.
The necessity to have more of those robots is because humans tends to make mistakes.
Making a mistake can vary in consequences.

Sometimes the mistake is trivial and thus result in no injury or issue on the environment or people occupying it.
But sometimes, those mistakes leads to fatality or environmental catastrophe.
It is precisely for the former reasons that we need those autonomous robots.
Because of their ability to repeat the same action without risk of major issue, autonomous robots are perfect to 
replace humans in those specific areas.

This will have an impact on different sectors.
From being able to build house or buildings faster, cheaper and respectful of all the different rules and regulations;
to be able to transport goods from point A to point B without any accident, the applications or fields of intervention of
those robots are large enough to justify their presence and to heavily rely on them in the future.
But what about the people's job those robots are going to replace?
What can we do about it and how this change will affect people jobs prospect in the future.
