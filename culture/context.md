## For the convenience of all

Let's begin with some quote I heard from someone I don't remember the name:
> Japanese culture is a culture of high context, while American culture is a culture of low context.

In this quote high and low don't respectively means better and lesser in terms of value.
It just indicate how important knowing the context in which you are in dictates your actions and words.

High context as that man explained once, means that you should be highly aware of the context in which you are in
and you should match your words and actions accordingly.
For example, it might be rare to find people talking loudly in a public train because the context indicates that this
is a space where we are silent until we arrive at destination.
If someone does it, it simply means that person is not aware of the context and thus makes things inconvenient for everyone.
If it is a foreigner, that is acceptable because we (all the people aware of the context) know that he doesn't know.
If it is a Japanese, maybe he is having a bad day or an urgent matter to deal with and therefore needs to break that rule.
Being aware of the context and acting accordingly makes things convenient for everyone, yes convenience is the goal.

Convenience means that things flow naturally, they are easy to understand, easy to deal with, practical and nice to everyone.
Maybe that is why there are several convenience store in Japan.

So, when you are in a situation in Japan, try to think about how convenient my actions and words should be in that particular context.
For example, taking some vacation is preferable to leaving the company altogether.
Because the later is inconvenient while the former makes things a bit difficult for your colleagues, it is less problematic.

That is also why several operations are highly contextualized.
To do something, first find out in what context that thing is allowed before doing it.
Even if you see something that may suggest otherwise, please think about the context in which that thing is appropriate.
Also, take into account that because the Japanese culture is how it is, deceptive behavior is not to be expected.
Basically, take things at face value in respect to their context.

## Context is superior to emotion

In this highly contextualized sociaty, emotion takes a backseat compared to other society.
Acting and speaking according to the context is the best course of action rather than simply following your own emotion.
Your emotion may say something, but in Japan context is key and what the context dictates in terms of actions and words should be prioritized above all.

It is difficult at first because the rest of the world may not act similarly, but this is Japan and to fit in you have to act according to Japan's worldview.
But emotion has value.

In some context, emotion are necessary and should be displayed.
Joy should be channelled in some context.
Affection similarly.
Compassion also.

It is using the emotion rather than being controlled by them that is effective in Japan.
It sound strange but it is what it is.

Because of that, it can lead to some misunderstanding.
Someone with a high context culture may do something to someone with a low context culture and misunderstanding can rise.
That is why understanding the other person culture is important.

You have to know the context and when you don't know, you have to decipher the context in which you are supposed to opperate.

## People are honnest and happy to help you

In Japan, I learn that 99.99% of the people I have met are honnest and happy to help you.
They don't need to have their context or mindset changed.
They are honnest, therefore, it is rare to see them trying to decieve you or to trick you.
If you are in trouble, they are happy to help you.

Basically, because the society is highly contextualized, there isn't a space for you to be lost (if you know the context)
or in trouble.

You just have to be like them, be honnest, happy to help and know the context in which you are supposed to operate and 
everything will be fine.

## Checklist

This is a short checklist that helps me to navigate the Japanese culture safely.
While I am still learning a lot of things, it has been very helpful.

1. Know the context in which you are.
2. Know the action this context allows.
3. Know the words this context allows.
4. Act and speak according to this context.
5. Repeat.
